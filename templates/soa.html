About Service Oriented Architecture (SOA)

Defining SOA

  • Services are individual, self contained functions that are independent of external fluctuations.
  • Services can ideally be reused in multiple systems with little to no changes to the service itself.
  • SOA emphasizes loose coupling between services -- constructing them such that they are versatile and are capable of being reused, regardless of software or requesters involved.

SOA in this web application

  • In this web application, "main.py" acts as an Enterprise Service Bus (ESB) by calling upon other services.
  • Namely, it calls upon Flask services to render html webpages for each link, and it calls upon "foaf_parse.py" to perform the SPARQL query of the foaf.rdf file
  • Flask and foaf_parse are services since they can be reused in other systems without changing their internal logic.
